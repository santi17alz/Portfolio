# AI Safety as a Public Security Requirement

> A research paper arguing that frontier AI safety can no longer be
> entrusted to the companies building these systems — and must instead
> be independently audited and legally mandated.

**ECE / Security Engineering Research · University of Massachusetts Amherst · Spring 2026**  
*Santiago Alzate*

---

## Abstract

This paper examines the security implications of increasingly capable AI systems through
the lens of safety, accountability, and dual-use risk. It argues that AI safety cannot be
treated as a corporate promise when commercial incentives consistently reward speed and
market dominance over careful testing and enforceable safeguards. Through case studies
including Claude Mythos Preview and AI-assisted cyberattacks, it makes the case that AI
safety should be treated as a public security requirement — in the same way pharmaceutical
trials and financial audits are.

**Keywords:** AI safety · AI regulation · dual-use technology · frontier model security

---

## Themes

- **Dual-use AI in cybersecurity** — the same model capabilities that help defenders find
  and patch vulnerabilities also help attackers discover and exploit them (Claude Mythos case study)
- **The criminal AI ecosystem** — jailbroken and uncensored models already circulating
  in cybercriminal markets
- **Surveillance and state power** — Palantir and the expansion of AI-driven systems into
  policing, defense, and intelligence
- **Philosophical stakes** — what increasingly capable AI means for human learning,
  writing, judgment, and purpose

## Argument

AI safety cannot depend on the intentions of the companies building frontier models.
This paper calls for: independent audits of frontier AI systems, legally enforceable
deployment standards, clear liability when AI contributes to harm, and stronger public
oversight in security-critical domains.
