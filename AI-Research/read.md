**Santiago Alzate Echeverry**  
University of Massachusetts Amherst · Spring 2026  
Keywords: `AI safety` · `AI regulation` · `dual-use technology` · `frontier model security`

---

## Abstract

This paper examines the security implications of increasingly capable AI systems through
the lens of safety, accountability, and dual-use risk. It argues that AI safety can no longer
be entrusted to the companies designing these systems — commercial incentives consistently
reward speed, performance, and market dominance over careful testing and enforceable
safeguards. Through case studies including Claude Mythos Preview and AI-assisted
cyberattacks, it makes the case that AI safety should be treated as a public security
requirement rather than a corporate promise.

## Central Argument

AI safety cannot depend on corporate promises. This paper calls for:

- **Independent audits** of frontier AI systems
- **Legally enforceable standards** for all deployments
- **Clear liability** when AI systems contribute to harm
- **Public oversight** in security-critical domains
- **Transparency** from frontier AI companies

The analogy: pharmaceutical trials don't mean society opposes medicine. They mean
society understands that systems this consequential must be tested and held accountable
before the public is asked to trust them.
