# Personal Statement

**Santiago Alzate Echeverry**
Computer Engineering · University of Massachusetts Amherst
Focus: Cybersecurity · AI Security · Hardware & Embedded Security

---

I'm a Computer Engineer drawn to cybersecurity by a simple conviction: the systems the modern world runs on are only as trustworthy as they are secure, and that security is too often underestimated. Nearly everything we build depends on keeping out unauthorized access, and as AI becomes embedded in our most critical systems, that work has never mattered more. The intersection of AI and security is where I've focused much of my own effort, including research I wrote on the security implications of increasingly capable AI.

My technical foundation spans programming (C/C++, Python, SQL, Bash), threat modeling, cryptography, and reverse engineering. I've applied all of it hands-on. I designed and built a hardware authentication token grounded in cryptographic design and a full threat model, reverse-engineered an undocumented hardware controller from the ground up, and automated systems in a critical-infrastructure environment during my internship at Schweitzer Engineering Laboratories. Across all three of my senior-year projects, I also took on a leadership role: organizing the team, running our meetings prepared with clear goals, checking in on progress, and delegating work to keep us moving.

Above all, I'm guided by integrity and a strong ethical code. I want to protect users' privacy and data, and to work for an organization that genuinely values the people it serves rather than treating them as numbers. Security work, to me, is fundamentally about protecting people, and I want to bring my technical foundation, my leadership, and my commitment to doing the work honestly to a team where I can help defend the systems people rely on every day.
